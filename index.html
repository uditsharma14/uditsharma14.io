About the Visualization

Messaging

COVID-19 affected countries across the globe, but the intensity, timing, and scale of its impact varied significantly by region.This visualization begins by unpacking the timeline of daily cases worldwide and then shifts focus to spatial differences using a continent-filterable world map. Together, they highlight how the pandemic evolved both over time and across geographies.

Narrative Structure

This narrative visualization follows a Martini Glass structure, beginning with a linear, guided story and transitioning into user interaction.

  • First Chart introduces the progression of the pandemic in USA with a bar chart showing major waves like overall Covid Progression, First Wave and Omicron.
  • Second Chart 2 shifts to a choropleth map of country-level impact with a continent filter for spatial exploration.
  • While guided, both scenes allow light interaction (playback, filters), setting up the story for further exploratory visuals in later scenes.

Visual Structure

  • Each scene includes a clear title, central interactive chart, and narrative block placed above or beside the chart.
  • Dropdowns, legends, and tooltips are consistently styled across both scenes to maintain cohesion.
  • Color encoding strategies include:
    • Bar Chart: Blue bars for daily case volume over time.
    • Choropleth Map: d3.interpolateReds for cumulative cases per country.
    • Selected continents remain vivid while others dim, focusing user attention on filtered regions.
